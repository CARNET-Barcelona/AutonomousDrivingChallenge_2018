THE ADC

 

The SEAT Autonomous Driving Challenge is a competition aimed at students with technical background in robotics that are going to develop future car technology. The participants develop fully automatic driving functions and the necessary software architectures. The important thing is that this development is applicable and scalable to real-life autonomous driving algorithms and provides students with the ability to have a part in creating this mobility concept for the future.

The project will officially kick-off in May, when the students will receive their model cars and get immerse in a two-day Techpills from robotic experts to ensure that all participants have an equal starting point. Then, students receive 1:10 scale model cars built at FU Berlin and feature an integrated CPU unit, a set of sensors and equipped with integrated LINUX Robotic OS Library systems.

 

The student teams will have 6 months to develop their software solution that will eventually allow the cars to autonomously navigate a road course. During this time, they will receive mentoring and perform intermediate tasks to ensure their development along the way. All this in an innovative and interactive environment connected with other teams through the Driving Challenge website and Slack Forum.

It is time for the cars to prove that they can indeed drive autonomously.

 

The final event will be the time to show their knowledge and effort, testing their cars in different scenarios, which have been derived from requirements arising from a realistic environment. All teams will meet again and showcase the result of the extremely complex work that they have completed.

 

The cars are expected to pass a set of challenges on a road course specifically constructed for the event. The cars will need to be able to park, navigate the course, avoid obstacles and observe and react to traffic lights within a city environment akin to cities such as Barcelona. The overall winner can then be determined by calculating the total number of points earned in all disciplines.

FINAL EVENT

 

CHALLENGES

Vehicles are expected to complete a total of three challenges with increasing difficulty per challenge:

For the first challenge, the cars need to complete 2-3 laps of the track fully autonomously. They need to showcase that they can find their way around, stay inside of lanes and navigate curves, straights and intersections. A time limit will be given for the vehicle to complete the number of laps.

 

In the second challenge, the vehicle will complete the track again. This time, a number of both, static and moving obstacles are placed on the track. In this challenge, the vehicle needs to demonstrate its ability to detect and navigate around such obstacles.

The final and most demanding challenge combines all aspects of challenge two and one with the added difficulty of alternating traffic lights and different parking situations that the vehicle needs to complete. Advisory juries will now also be looking for the vehicle missing traffic lights and failing to complete the parking procedure.
